Introduction¶

This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row.\ ScheduledDay tells us on what day the patient set up their appointment.\ Neighborhood indicates the location of the hospital.\ Scholarship indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Famlia.\ Be careful about the encoding of the last column: it says No if the patient showed up to their appointment, and Yes if they did not show up.

Questions to be answered¶

Q1: Can age affect showing at the appointments\ Q2: Can condition affect why indivduals might not show up for their appointments?\ Q3: does gender affect why indivduals might not show up for their appointments\ Q4: Does The waiting days affect on showing to the appointment\ Q5: What are the top and least neighborhoods to no show up for their appointments\ Q6: Does not recieving sms affect not showing to the appointments

Conclusion¶

According to the data and stats we explored :

Neighbourhood Hospitals varies between not showing to the appointments as some neighborhood have higher not showing rates than others,further analysis is required to answer specific neighbourhood questions.

Age:\ Patients were classified by age_category, we can notice that age categories (20 to 40 and 40 to 60 ) have the highest count of showing to the appointments and they are the same age group with the highest count of not showing up,other variable might affect that age group but are not available in the dataset.

Conditions:\ Histograms in noshow & show dataframe showed that [Hypertension ,Diabetes, Alcoholism & Handicap] are similar , patient conditions are not the main cause for not showing up.

Gender:
65% of the entries are Female and 35% are Male which might lead to a skewed results if we explored their condition

sms recieved:
Clients who received SMS is 22278 vs who didn't receive SMS is 88049 with the following diffrences:

we can notice that those who received SMS about their appointments represents 32% of total appointments, patients who showed up to their appointments only 29% of them received SMS while 71% didn't receive it but still showed up, Those who didn't show up received a higher rate of SMS at 43.8%.

waiting days:
have negative effect on showing to the appointment but not a large effect \ Final conclusion in order to predict whether the patients are going to show up or not, we will need more data like (Income , Marital status, kids, Job type)
